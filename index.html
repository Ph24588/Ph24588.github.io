Portfolio Blog

Hello, my name is Paul Hwang

I am a young graphic designer living in Atlanta. I have experience in branding, front-end, and many other categories. However, user interface design is my expertise.
Learning and building is something I have been doing all my life. It has been about 5 years since I started learning graphic design. I primarily work with Adobe software (Photoshop, Illustrator, Indesign, Lightroom) and I dabble a bit in HTML and CSS as well. I love keeping up with the latest trends in design and technology, exploring new mediums, and meeting other motivated, self-starting designers.
I love cooking, listening to music, and cruising around on longboards. Currently, I am enrolled at the University of Georgia as an Intended Graphic Design major.

Take a look at what I have been doing

NightLive is a social mobile application that lets you connect with people at your local bars and your local downtown scene. During the first development stage, I will be working closely with the developers, designing the mobile application and branding.

UGAHacks is a hackathon at the University of Georgia that wants to cultivate the passion and technical abilities of students with the maker mentality. As the teams designer, I am designing everything from the site, to the stationary.

I am currently interning at Cooleaf as a designer, designing mobile interfaces. Based in Atlanta Tech Village, they work to promote employee engagement with their application, which is an easy-to-use platform to manage, track, and incentivize your 'people programs' while eliminating the clutter of shared calendars and email.

Bitmo takes a new look at virtual bitcoin exchange and offers users to easily convert dollars to Bitcoin, and vice versa. With it approaching release, I have been focusing on developing the UI and brand with the founder and developer, Logan Allen. We presented at Startup Riot.

While interning at Spectracom Group, a company focused on ecommerce, I was involved in a variety of projects. With my focus being design/marketing at SPG, I had many opportunities to delve into fields, like advertising, branding, communications, social media, and UI/UX. Specifically, I worked on projects like redesigning the website UI, writing extensive blog posts, designing email blasts, and updating the SPG brand.

Helping Hands is a proof of concept application that would instantly connect you with a technology specialist and assist you with general technological issues relating to phones and computers with the press of a button. It was created with the idea of assisting the elderly who aren’t familiar with new technologies. While designing the mobile app and site UI, I worked closely with the developer and business team.

At a hackathon at Emory, a few developers and I created Rhythm Route, a proof of concept application that pairs your favorite tunes with jogging routes to create a unique experience. When designing the UI/UX for the application, I worked with the developers to understand the capabilities of the APIs we implemented, Spotify and Endomondo. create routes and the Spotify API to create playlists and select songs for the routes.

Nudge, a working web application that uses Ebays API to generate a few random questions to allow others to select a fun and creative gift for others to purchase, was developed at HackGT. During the 30 hours of hacking, the developers created to front-end, while I designed the brand and the UI. Nudge won the Ebay API prize and our team placed as one of the top ten hacks at the event.
